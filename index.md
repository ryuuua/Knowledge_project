<section id="ongoing-projects" class="card reveal delay-2">
  <div class="section-head">
    <p class="section-label">Now Running</p>
    <h2>Ongoing Projects</h2>
  </div>

  <div class="project-board">
    <article class="project-panel reveal delay-3">
      <div class="project-top">
        <h3>CEBRA-NLP-gen2</h3>
        <span class="status">In Progress</span>
      </div>
      <p>
        CEBRA-NLP-gen2 develops a next-generation framework for mapping internal
        language-model representations into temporally coherent knowledge spaces,
        with emphasis on reproducible training protocols and robust evaluation.
      </p>
      <ul class="project-list">
        <li>Representation alignment: stabilization of latent trajectories</li>
        <li>Evaluation protocol: quantitative consistency against external knowledge</li>
        <li>Reproducibility assets: structured release of experiment logs and settings</li>
      </ul>
    </article>

    <article class="project-panel reveal delay-4">
      <div class="project-top">
        <h3>Larm liniditip</h3>
        <span class="status">In Progress</span>
      </div>
      <p>
        Larm liniditip investigates how long-horizon knowledge can be transformed
        into lightweight inference pathways while preserving interpretability
        and practical responsiveness under deployment constraints.
      </p>
      <ul class="project-list">
        <li>Memory retrieval: reintegration of information beyond the context window</li>
        <li>Inference pathway: validation of linearized reasoning traces</li>
        <li>Operational readiness: low-latency stress and stability assessment</li>
      </ul>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section id="focus" class="grid-2 reveal delay-3">
  <article class="card">
    <div class="section-head compact">
      <p class="section-label">Research Focus</p>
      <h2>Core Direction</h2>
    </div>
    <p>
      Our central research question concerns where knowledge is formed,
      how it is preserved over time, and when it is invoked during inference.
      We design measurable systems that integrate predictive performance,
      evidential grounding, and cross-task reusability.
    </p>
    <ul class="mono-list">
      <li>Knowledge acquisition and retention modeling</li>
      <li>Memory-aware generation control</li>
      <li>Human-interpretable output protocol</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
